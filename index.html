The Problem

By the time a traditional system activates, the fire has already spread.

Smoke detectors, heat sensors and sprinklers all share the same weakness: they are reactive. They wait for a fire to grow large enough to trigger a response — and in enclosed, high-density risk points like battery cabinets, switchboards and cable trays, that delay is often the difference between a contained incident and a catastrophic loss.

Electrical faults and product defects remain leading causes of large-scale fires across Singapore and the region, from data centre battery rooms to EV manufacturing lines — precisely the enclosed, hard-to-reach spaces where conventional detection struggles most.

The Answer

An active layer of defence, built into the material itself

AEGIS suspends liquid FK-5-1-12 fire suppressant inside a non-porous, temperature-sensitive polymer shell — turning a liquid extinguisher into a solid, static, always-ready state.

🌡️

1. Heat compromises the shell

Fire heat weakens the capsule's polymer wall. No detector, no signal — the material itself is the sensor.

💥

2. Internal pressure ruptures it

FK-5-1-12 vaporises inside the shell, pressure rises, and the capsule bursts open automatically at the exact point of heat.

❄️

3. Cooling + oxygen displacement

Released agent absorbs heat below combustion temperature and displaces oxygen below the 15% threshold fire needs to sustain itself.
